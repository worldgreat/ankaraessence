Our Journey

From a small Lagos workshop to a global sustainable fashion brand, we're redefining African fashion for the modern world.

Roots in Tradition

Ankara Essence was born in 2025 from a simple idea: to honor traditional Nigerian textile art while making it relevant for today's global citizens. Our founder, Obamadeji Ebunoluwa, grew up watching her grandmother weave beautiful Aso Oke fabrics in their family compound in Ibadan.

Ebunoluwa studied fashion design in London but always felt pulled back to the vibrant colors and patterns of her childhood. She noticed that while African prints were becoming popular worldwide, much of the production was moving away from Africa, taking opportunities away from local artisans.

With just ₦500,000 in savings and a determination to keep production local, she returned to Lagos and set up our first small workshop.

The Sustainable Turn

we made a pivotal decision that would define our brand. After visiting several textile factories and seeing the environmental impact firsthand, we committed to 100% sustainable practices.

We partnered with organic cotton farmers in northern Nigeria, developed natural dyeing techniques using indigenous plants, and implemented a zero-waste production system where every fabric scrap is repurposed.

This wasn't just about being eco-friendly—it was about returning to the ways our ancestors practiced textile arts: in harmony with nature, valuing quality over quantity, and creating pieces meant to last generations.

Bridging Continents

Today, Ankara Essence bridges African heritage with global fashion consciousness. While our production remains entirely in Nigeria, our designs speak to Africans in the diaspora who want to stay connected to their roots through what they wear.

We've grown from a one-woman operation to a team of 45 talented artisans, designers, and sustainability experts. Each piece in our collection tells a story—of the hands that made it, the traditional techniques preserved, and the modern aesthetic it represents.

Our London office handles European distribution, allowing us to maintain our commitment to keeping production and its economic benefits in Africa while serving our international community.

Our Guiding Principles

Cultural Integrity

We honor traditional Nigerian textile arts while innovating for contemporary wearability. Every pattern has meaning and history.

Sustainable Production

From organic materials to zero-waste techniques, we're committed to fashion that respects both people and planet.

Artisan Empowerment

We provide fair wages, skills training, and stable employment to Nigerian artisans, keeping valuable craftsmanship alive.
